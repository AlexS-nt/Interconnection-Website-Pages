Impacts of Tourism on the Great Barrier Reef

Tourist can have a large negative impact on the Great Barrier Reef. Here are some examples:

  • Physical Damage: Tourists can often touch coral damaging it and sometimes killing it
  • Chemical Damage: Chemicals can be released from sunscreen, which can be harmful to coral
  • Environmental Impact: People generate waste, which can include containers and general rubbish, which can hurt and damage the aquatic life

Not only is there negative impacts, there are some positive impacts, here are some

  • Economic Benefits: Tourism generates a large amount of income, which can be used to fund other options for gaining money in tourism
  • Employment Opportunities: Tourism can open job opportunities for local communities, which supplies them with a stable income.
  • Conservation Funding: Income from tourism can be used for funding on research, for example funding for research on the coral reef.
  • Environmental Awareness: Tourism can help raise awareness about the importance of preserving the Great Barrier Reef.
